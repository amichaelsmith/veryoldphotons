Galleries

Galaxies

Islands of light in an overwhelmingly dark universe, most galaxies consist of billions of stars. The galaxies presented here lie at distances ranging from 2 million to 200 million light years.

Nebulae

Majestic clouds of hydrogen gas with traces of common elements such as oxygen, sulfur and nitrogen. Abundant in our own Milky Way galaxy, they are birthplace of future generations of stars.

Star Clusters

Communities of stars bound by gravity, clusters range from sparse young galactic (open) clusters to dense ancient globular clusters.

Solar System

Our own cosmic neighborhood presents a rich menagerie of planets, moons, comets, and asteroids.
